A beautifully mounted TV with cables dangling down the wall is one of the most common disappointments after a DIY install. Fortunately, hiding TV wires is straightforward when done professionally — and the difference it makes to the look of your room is immediate.

Here is how we handle cable concealment in Nashville homes, and what method works best for each wall type.

---

## Method 1: In-Wall Routing (The Cleanest Option)

In-wall routing runs your TV cables **completely inside the wall** using a power bridge kit. From the outside, you see nothing — just a blank wall and a floating TV.

**How it works:**
1. We cut two small openings in the drywall — one behind the TV and one near the nearest outlet below
2. We fish a power bridge cable through the wall cavity
3. Your HDMI, power, and other cables run through the same path
4. We install flush-mount wall plates at both openings — one behind the TV, one near the floor
5. The TV plugs in cleanly at the top and the power exits cleanly at the bottom

**Best for:** Standard drywall walls in living rooms, bedrooms, and media rooms. Works in most Nashville homes built in the last 30 years.

**Cost:** $60 add-on per TV when combined with a mounting appointment.

## Method 2: Surface Raceway (For Hard Walls and Rentals)

A surface raceway is a slim plastic or aluminum channel that mounts on the wall surface and covers your cables. It can be **painted to match your wall color**, making it nearly invisible from a normal viewing distance.

**Best for:**
- Brick, stone, tile, or concrete walls where drilling through is not practical
- Apartments and rentals where you need a damage-free option
- Fireplace surrounds where cables need to run vertically along the side

**Cost:** Also included in the $60 cable concealment add-on.

If you are not sure, we assess the wall during the appointment and recommend the right approach before starting.

## What Cables Can Be Concealed?

All of them:
- **Power cable** (most important — the thickest one)
- **HDMI** from cable box, streaming stick, game console
- **Optical audio** to a soundbar
- **Ethernet** for hardwired smart TV connections

We route all cables together in a single clean path so nothing hangs loose.

## The Fireplace Wall Challenge

Fireplace walls in Nashville homes are some of the most requested cable concealment jobs we do. The challenge is routing cables from a TV mounted 5–6 feet up to devices sitting below on a console or inside a cabinet.

On drywall fireplace surrounds, in-wall routing works great. On brick or stone, a raceway running vertically along the edge of the fireplace and painted to match looks very clean.
